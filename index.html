About me

I am a PhD candidate in Civil Engineering at the University of Arkansas (expected May 2026), with an academic background in urban and regional planning and transportation systems. Prior to starting my PhD, I served as a faculty member at the Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET), where I taught undergraduate and graduate courses in transportation planning, traffic engineering, statistics, GIS, and participatory planning, supervised student theses, and contributed to curriculum development and accreditation-related work.

My research interests sit at the intersection of travel behavior, transportation equity, and multimodal accessibility—especially in small and resource-constrained cities. I study how people perceive transportation challenges (for example, the usability of transit, sidewalks, and bike infrastructure) and how those perceptions influence mode choice and car dependence. I also work on shared micromobility planning and evaluation, including community-centered bikeshare design, adoption, and the accessibility impacts of bikeshare–walk–transit connections.
